UX Case Study

Wallet Wiz is a microsite that gives users a money personality test. Once finished it gives personalized financial advice and suggested banking products that will improve the user's overall financial health.

Overview

The growing popularity of digital banking and fintech companies have resulted in the breakdown of trust that younger generations have with traditional banking. Banks are not keeping up with the demands of more personalized experiences and inclusivity. The goal of this study is to increase customer engagement in young adults by catering to their curiosity for self-discovery and self-improvement. This design is a mobile app quiz that gives a deeper understanding of its user's personalities around money and provides personalized financial solutions that increase their engagement and overall financial health. By taking this quiz, users will gain insight and awareness that will point them in the right direction to tools and services that will further improve their financial well-being.

Problem/challenge

The challenge is creating a digital experience that can analyze a user’s current behavior and engagement level with their prospective financial institutions. Doing so gives them recommendations to key banking products and services that will benefit their overall financial health.

Solution

This app aims to tackle the challenges that young adults often face in the banking industry. These challenges revolve around enhancing financial well-being, offering tailored advice, and addressing trust issues in banking. By honing in on the fundamental habits and behaviors that impact users' financial health, the app takes a holistic approach to delivering personalized solutions, directly addressing these pain points.

Interviews

I interviewed 12 millennials over the course of 3 days to gain insight into their current financial health and how they handle their money. I wanted to gauge how they felt about money from different perspectives. While conducting the interviews I had 4 goals that I kept in mind.

  1. To determine how engaged user are with their finances.
  2. How important is financial education?
  3. What steps have they taken to improve financial health?
  4. What’s holding them back from achieving their financial goals?

Insights

The biggest takeaway from my initial research is that young adults want to improve their overall financial well-being. They look at financial stability from more of a holistic view, not just earning a high income. There’s also investing, saving for retirement, and being prepared for uncertain emergencies. This explains the rise in fintech and non-banking app usage because these different channels can be used in tandem to target different financial needs.

User Personas

Sam

Sam is a budget-conscious individual who meticulously looks for discounts, coupons, and cashback offers to maximize their income. This financial prudence extends to savings for short-term goals like a vacation and long-term aspirations such as buying a home. Notably, Sam is committed to financial education, expressing a strong desire to delve deeper into the intricacies of investing. Sam’s tech-savvy behavior reflects not only a modern lifestyle but also a strategic use of digital tools to manage finances and stay informed about investment options. Despite his smart decision-making and his disciplined attitude, Sam can get really anxious about what to do for future financial success. He knows saving isn’t enough and that worries him often.

Background

Frustrations

Goals

  • Seeks personalized tips on optimizing everyday spending to save more.
  • Interested in beginner-friendly investment guidance.

Alex

Alex is a go-with-the-flow kind of person who loves diving into new ideas and opportunities. Life for Alex is a bit like a rollercoaster, thanks to project-based work that brings in income on its own schedule. Despite the unpredictable cash flow, Alex keeps trying out different financial strategies to handle the ups and downs, but with little success. Building up an emergency fund is a top priority for Alex – a safety net for those times when projects are between paychecks. Short-term financial goals are the name of the game, fitting right in with the freelance lifestyle of tackling one project at a time. Despite his love of freelance, the stress of this inconsistent lifestyle is really weighing down his mental health. Alex wants to make changes but is overwhelmed about what to do.
